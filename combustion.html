What is an Internal Combustion Car?

An internal combustion car is a automobile that uses gasoline inorder to function. The process consists of the combustion of air (usually Oxygen) and fuel (gasoline/diesel) in order to combust a reaction.

Why are internal combustion cars so common?

Combustion cars are so common because they are the most developed form of transporation. Ever since the 1980s, people like Carl Benz have created influencial innovations on how combustion engines function, whilst electric motors have only been usable in more recent times.

What are some of the current advantages of using gasoline cars instead of electric powered cars?

Cost is a major reason of why gasoline cars are so much more known around the globe. On average you would save about $20,000 if you were to get a "normal" car instead of an electric one.
